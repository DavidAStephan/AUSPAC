Handoff note. Diagnoses the persistent ~11Q / ~14Q oscillation in the AU-PAC
IRFs and documents the fix applied to `dynare/au_pac.mod`. The prior §6.10 work

## TL;DR

The persistent oscillation in the IRFs is **not** from the PAC
consumption/housing/employment/BI blocks that §6.10 tuned. It is generated by
the **trade error-correction blocks** — exports (`dln_x`) and non-energy imports
(`dln_m_ne`). Their AU-OLS growth-persistence coefficients (`b1_x = 0.8673`,
`b1_m_ne = 0.7427`) put complex eigenvalues at modulus `√b1 ≈ 0.93 / 0.86`,
right next to the unit circle, so the cycle barely damps over 40 quarters.

**Fix applied:** reverted `b1_x → 0.30` and `b1_m_ne → 0.23` (wp1044 reference).
Oscillation eliminated, model stays Blanchard–Kahn stable.

## Root cause

Both trade blocks have the same structure (`au_pac.mod` ~L1353–1410):

```
ln_x_level = ln_x_level(-1) + dln_x                 // level = running sum of growth (integrator)
dln_x      = b0_x*x_gap(-1) + b1_x*dln_x(-1) + …    // growth = error-correction + AR momentum
x_gap      = ln_x_eq - ln_x_level                   // gap = equilibrium − level
```

Substituting collapses to a second-order difference equation in the level:

```
L_t = (1 − b0 + b1)·L_{t-1} − b1·L_{t-2}
```

whose complex roots have **modulus exactly √b1**. High growth-persistence `b1`
therefore pushes the roots toward the unit circle. This is the classic
momentum-ECM / Samuelson multiplier–accelerator oscillator: growth has inertia,
so when the level overshoots equilibrium the gap reverses growth but momentum
carries it past → it overshoots the other way.

Eigenvector decomposition of the 126×126 state-transition matrix confirms it:

`m_ne_gap`, `ln_ib_level`. **`yhat_au` participation is only 0.149** — it is
driven by the cycle, not the source of it.

## Why §6.10 missed it

§6.10 attributed the cycle to "PAC-block ↔ demand-identity feedback" and reduced
the *loadings* (`h_pac_*`, `b3_ih`, `b5_n`). That shrinks amplitude but cannot
change the eigenvalues. Direct test (perturbing each candidate and re-solving):

| Experiment | 11Q mode after |
|------------|----------------|
| `b3_ib = 0` (BI accelerator off) | 0.9314 — **unchanged** |
| all `b3 = 0` (no contemp demand loop) | 0.9314 — **unchanged** |
| `lambda_i = 0.50` (less policy inertia) | 0.9329 — **unchanged** |
| all `b0 × 4` (faster PAC ECM) | 0.9316 — **unchanged** |
| `b1_x → 0.30` | **mode gone** (collapses to \|λ\|≈0.55) |

The dominant mode is invariant to everything §6.10 touched and moves only with
`b1_x`. The §6.9/§6.10 working-paper prose should be corrected accordingly.

## Context: is 0.87 a real number?

`b1_x = 0.8673` / `b1_m_ne = 0.7427` are the AU single-equation OLS estimates;
wp1044 used 0.30 / 0.23. A quarterly export-growth AR(1) of 0.87 is very high and
most likely reflects a near-unit-root / unremoved trend in the growth series
(small sample, omitted deterministic trend, or genuine persistence). If you want
to defend keeping some AU signal rather than a full revert, shrinkage
`b1 = w·AU + (1−w)·wp1044` works cleanly — `w=0.5` already gives half-life ~2.5Q
(1–2 cycles then flat). We chose the full revert (`w=0`).
